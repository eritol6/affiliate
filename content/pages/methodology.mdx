# Our Methodology

The Buyers Reports evaluates compact home fitness equipment using a structured scoring system. The framework is designed for buyers comparing options in constrained training environments, especially apartment and small-space setups.

Our analysis prioritizes small-space usability, durability expectations, and long-term value under realistic ownership conditions. This publication is affiliate-supported, but rankings are determined independently through the criteria below.

## Evaluation Criteria

### Footprint Efficiency
This measures how much usable room a product requires once setup and movement clearance are included. Base dimensions alone are not enough. Vertical and horizontal space use are both considered.

### Build Quality & Durability
This reviews materials, structural stability, and mechanical complexity. Simpler mechanisms can reduce long-term failure points in some categories. Durability expectations are assessed relative to intended use.

### Resistance System
This compares stacks, plate-loaded systems, and digital resistance approaches. Smoothness, adjustment speed, and practical progression all matter. The goal is to identify systems that remain useful over time.

### Adjustment & Ease of Use
This measures setup friction during real sessions. Adjustment workflow, learning curve, and day-to-day usability are weighted heavily for apartment-friendly use cases. Slower systems can reduce training consistency.

### Value Per Dollar
This evaluates features against price, upgrade potential, and expected lifespan. Lower cost is not automatically better if long-term utility is weak. Value is measured over ownership, not initial checkout only.

## Scoring Framework

Each product receives an internal score on a 10-point scale. Category weights are adjusted by product type, and scores are comparative within each category rather than universal across all equipment classes.

- Footprint: 20%
- Durability: 25%
- Resistance System: 25%
- Ease of Use: 15%
- Value: 15%

## What We Prioritize

- Small-space practicality
- Apartment-friendly setups
- Equipment that can scale with progression
- Long-term usability over trend features
